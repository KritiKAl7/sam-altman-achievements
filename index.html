Early Life & Education

Born on April 22, 1985, in St. Louis, Missouri, Sam Altman showed an early interest in technology, receiving his first computer at the age of eight. He attended John Burroughs School, where he developed his passion for programming and entrepreneurship.

Altman went on to study computer science at Stanford University but dropped out to pursue his entrepreneurial ambitions. Despite not completing his formal education, his contributions to technology were later recognized when he received an honorary degree from the University of Waterloo.

Y Combinator Era

2014

Appointed as President of Y Combinator, succeeding Paul Graham.

2015

Launched YC Continuity, a fund for supporting YC companies in later-stage growth.

2016

Established YC Research, a non-profit research lab focused on long-term innovation.

2019

Stepped down as President to focus on OpenAI, leaving behind a legacy of nurturing companies like Airbnb, Dropbox, Stripe, and Reddit.

OpenAI Leadership

As CEO of OpenAI, Sam Altman has been instrumental in transforming the organization into a leading AI research laboratory. He co-founded OpenAI in 2015 with a mission to ensure that artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity.

Under his leadership, OpenAI secured a landmark $10 billion investment from Microsoft and developed groundbreaking AI models including the GPT series and ChatGPT, which has become one of the fastest-growing consumer applications in history.

Altman has been a vocal advocate for responsible AI development, emphasizing the importance of safety and ethical considerations in the advancement of artificial intelligence technologies.

Other Ventures & Investments

Loopt

Founded by Altman at age 19, this location-based social networking app was acquired by Green Dot Corporation for $43.4 million in 2012.

Hydrazine Capital

A personal investment fund established by Altman to invest in promising startups.

Energy Investments

Significant investments in nuclear fusion company Helion Energy and advanced fission company Oklo, reflecting his interest in clean energy solutions.

Biotech

Invested in Retro Biosciences, a company focused on extending human healthspan through cellular reprogramming, epigenetic reprogramming, and autophagy.

Key Achievements & Impact

Sam Altman has emerged as one of the most influential figures in the technology industry, particularly in the field of artificial intelligence. His leadership at OpenAI has accelerated the development and adoption of AI technologies worldwide.

As of July 2025, his net worth is estimated between $1.7 billion and $2 billion, primarily derived from his successful investments rather than OpenAI equity.

Altman has been outspoken about AI's potential impact on society and the job market, advocating for policies like universal basic income to address potential disruptions while emphasizing AI's capacity to drive unprecedented economic growth and innovation.
